The AI Homebrew Club

A short deck about fear, lies, and folding chairs.

The problem

People are afraid of AI.

50%of U.S. adults are more concerned than excited about AI
10%are more excited than concerned
37%were more concerned back in 2021. The fear is growing.

Pew Research Center, June 2025

  • Afraid it will take their job.
  • Afraid they are already too far behind to start.
  • Afraid it is all a scam.
  • Afraid of looking dumb for asking.

The fear does not come from the technology. It comes from facing it alone.

The noise

And the loudest voices are selling something.

The hype

“AI will do everything. Your competitors already use it. Buy my tool, take my course, act now.”

Sold by people who profit from your urgency.

The doom

“AI will take everything. The jobs, the truth, the kids. Click to read more.”

Sold by people who profit from your attention.

Both stories cast you as a spectator. Neither survives a live demo by your neighbor.

The fix

Small rooms beat big lies.

This has happened before. In 1975 a mail‑order computer kit confused and scared plenty of people too. A few dozen hobbyists started meeting in a garage in Menlo Park, passed the schematics around, and showed each other what the thing could actually do. They called it the Homebrew Computer Club. Two members demoed a little machine there that became Apple.

Nobody in that room was afraid of the microprocessor. They were taking turns with it.

Why a club

You cannot watch your way out of fear.

See it

A neighbor demos what AI did for their real business this month. Not a keynote, not an ad. A person you know, with a shop you know.

Try it

You bring one real task and do it with help sitting next to you. Ten minutes in, the fear has nothing left to grab.

Say it out loud

What scared you, what surprised you, what you would actually try. Honest talk beats headlines every time.

Courses sell certainty. Headlines sell fear. A club gives you witnesses.

What we believe

AI is the great equalizer.

The greatest way to redistribute wealth is to increase participation in capitalism. Big companies win because they can afford a back office. AI hands that same back office to anyone with a truck, a storefront, or a license. The only thing standing between most people and that leverage is fear, and fear is exactly what a club is for.

  • Free at the door, always. No tiers, no tickets.
  • Show and tell over lectures. No slideware, no vendors.
  • Commerce grows out of community, not the reverse.

Pull up a chair.

“Give to help others.”

Fear shrinks in good company.
